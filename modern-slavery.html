Our Commitment

Midly Labs Inc. is committed to preventing modern slavery and human trafficking in all activities and supply chains. This statement is published in accordance with Section 54 of the UK Modern Slavery Act 2015 and covers the financial year ending December 31, 2025.

Modern slavery is a serious crime and a violation of fundamental human rights. It encompasses slavery, servitude, forced and compulsory labour, and human trafficking. Midly Labs Inc. has a zero-tolerance approach to modern slavery and human trafficking, whether in our own business operations or through our supply chain and business relationships.

We are committed to acting ethically and with integrity in all our business relationships, and to implementing and enforcing effective systems and controls to ensure modern slavery is not taking place anywhere in our own business or in our supply chain.

Business Structure & Operations

Midly Labs Inc. is a corporation incorporated under the laws of the State of Georgia, United States. Our registered address is 8735 Dunwoody Place, #6603, Atlanta, GA 30350.

We develop and operate Midly CLM, a contract lifecycle management platform powered by artificial intelligence. Our business is conducted entirely through digital channels; we do not operate any physical manufacturing, warehousing, or retail facilities.

Our workforce is small and consists primarily of:

  • Employees: Full-time and part-time employees located in the United States, all engaged under formal employment contracts and compensated at or above applicable minimum wage requirements
  • Independent contractors: Software engineers, designers, and specialist consultants engaged on project or retainer bases under written service agreements
  • No child labour: We do not employ anyone under the age of 18

All workers in our direct operations have the right to work in the jurisdiction where they are engaged, and we verify this through our hiring and onboarding processes.

Supply Chain

As a cloud-native SaaS company, Midly Labs does not have a traditional physical supply chain. We do not manufacture goods, manage warehouses, or engage in logistics. Our supply chain consists exclusively of technology services and professional service providers.

Our primary suppliers and service providers include:

  • Cloud infrastructure: Vercel (hosting and edge compute), Neon (managed PostgreSQL database), Wasabi (object storage)
  • AI services: Anthropic (AI model APIs for contract analysis)
  • Authentication & payments: Clerk (identity management), Stripe (payment processing)
  • Communications: Resend and Postmark (email delivery)
  • Analytics: PostHog (product analytics), Google Analytics 4, Microsoft Clarity
  • Professional services: Legal counsel, accounting, and specialist technology consultants

Our suppliers are established technology companies domiciled in the United States that are themselves subject to applicable labour and human rights laws. The nature of our supply chain — entirely software and professional services — presents a low inherent risk of modern slavery and human trafficking compared to industries involving physical manufacturing or raw materials.

Nevertheless, we remain vigilant to risks at all tiers of our supply chain, and we expect our suppliers to uphold the same high standards we set for ourselves.

Due Diligence

While the nature of our business means the risk of modern slavery in our supply chain is low, we take a proportionate approach to due diligence that includes:

Supplier Assessment

When engaging new suppliers, we assess their ethical practices and compliance posture. For significant suppliers, we review their published policies on labour standards, human rights, and modern slavery where applicable. We prefer to engage suppliers who have formal policies addressing these issues and who are transparent about their labour practices.

Contractor Vetting

All independent contractors engaged by Midly Labs are subject to a vetting process that includes:

  • Verification of identity and right to work in the relevant jurisdiction
  • Review of background and professional credentials
  • Written service agreements containing representations regarding lawful employment status and the absence of coercion or trafficking
  • Payment made directly to the contractor's verified bank account or payment platform at market rates

Escalation Process

Any employee, contractor, or third party who has concerns about modern slavery or human trafficking in connection with our business is encouraged to raise those concerns with our legal team at legal@midly.ai. We will investigate all concerns raised and take appropriate remedial action. We will not penalise anyone for raising concerns in good faith.

Relevant Policies

Midly Labs maintains the following internal policies that are relevant to our commitment to preventing modern slavery and upholding human rights:

  • Code of Conduct: Sets out the ethical standards we expect of all employees and contractors, including respect for human dignity and compliance with applicable labour laws
  • Supplier Code of Conduct: Communicated to significant suppliers; requires compliance with applicable laws, prohibition on forced or child labour, and fair wages
  • Whistleblower / Speak-Up Policy: Provides a confidential channel for employees and contractors to report concerns about unethical conduct, including potential modern slavery, without fear of retaliation
  • Hiring Policy: Requires verification of right to work and prohibits engagement with labour intermediaries who cannot demonstrate lawful and ethical labour practices

These policies are reviewed annually by leadership and updated as needed to reflect changes in applicable law and best practice guidance.

Training & Awareness

We believe that awareness and education are essential to preventing modern slavery. Our approach to training includes:

  • Onboarding: All new employees and contractors receive information about our commitment to preventing modern slavery as part of their onboarding materials, including how to recognise and report concerns
  • Annual refresher: Employees in roles with any supplier or contractor engagement responsibility receive annual awareness refreshers on modern slavery risks and our due diligence obligations
  • Leadership commitment: Senior leadership is briefed annually on our Modern Slavery Act obligations and the content of this statement prior to its publication

Given the low inherent risk profile of our business, we have determined that mandatory certification training across the entire organisation is not currently proportionate. We will reassess this as the company grows and as our supply chain evolves.

Measuring Effectiveness

We monitor the effectiveness of our approach to preventing modern slavery through the following key performance indicators:

  • Zero incidents: Number of identified instances of modern slavery or human trafficking in our operations or supply chain — our target is zero, and we are not aware of any such instances to date
  • Supplier coverage: Percentage of significant suppliers (by spend) assessed against labour standards criteria
  • Speak-up channel: Number of concerns raised through our reporting channel, and time to investigate and resolve
  • Policy reviews: Completion of annual policy review cycle
  • Training completion: Percentage of employees in scope for modern slavery awareness training who have completed it

If we identify any instances of modern slavery or human trafficking in our operations or supply chain, we will take immediate steps to address the situation, including terminating the relevant business relationship and, where appropriate, reporting the matter to the relevant authorities.

We will continue to review and improve our approach each year, and we will update this statement annually to reflect our progress.

Questions regarding this statement or our approach to modern slavery may be directed to legal@midly.ai.
